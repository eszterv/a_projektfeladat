About the Cape Wrath Trail

The Cape Wrath Trail is an unmarked and unofficial long-distance route stretching approximately 200 miles (321 km) from Fort William to Cape Wrath in Scotland. Due to its unofficial status, hikers often divide the trail into sections based on personal preferences and logistical considerations.

One common approach is to split the trail into two main sections:

Southern Section
Fort William to Strathcarron
Northern Section
Strathcarron to Cape Wrath

This division is reflected in resources such as the Harvey Maps, which separate the trail into southern and northern segments at Strathcarron.1

However, it's important to note that there is no universally agreed-upon point where the southern part ends and the northern part begins. Some hikers may choose different endpoints for logistical reasons or based on their specific route choices. The flexibility of the Cape Wrath Trail allows for such variations, and hikers are encouraged to plan their journey according to their own preferences and capabilities. The present site presents the section from Glenfinnan to Ullapool designed for 8 days.
